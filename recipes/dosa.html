Dosa

Description

Dosa is a classic South Indian crepe made from fermented lentil and rice batter. It's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Traditionally served with coconut chutney and sambar, dosas are a delightful breakfast or snack option.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup urad dal (skinned black gram)
  • 2 cups rice (regular raw rice or basmati rice)
  • 2 tablespoons chana dal (bengal gram)
  • ½ teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds, optional)
  • 2 tablespoons poha (flattened rice)
  • 1 teaspoon rock salt (or non-iodized salt)
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Steps

  1. Soak urad dal, rice, chana dal, fenugreek seeds, and poha separately for a few hours.
  2. Grind them into a smooth batter individually.
  3. Mix the batters together, add salt, and let it ferment overnight.
  4. Heat a non-stick tawa (griddle) and spread a ladleful of batter to make thin dosas.
  5. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges and cook until crispy.
  6. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
